# Contributing

This is a living field guide. The best contributions make it sharper or more honest.

## What we want

- **Receipts.** Primary or peer-reviewed sources on context rot, long-context degradation,
  compression, or runtime/code-based retrieval. Add them to [docs/receipts.md](docs/receipts.md).
- **Counterexamples.** Results where compression *does* recover long-context accuracy, or where
  big context genuinely beats a subtracted one. The thesis is falsifiable on purpose — pressure
  it. Strong counterexamples get their own section.
- **Subtraction patterns.** Concrete, reproducible techniques for putting less in front of the model.
- **War stories.** Short, specific accounts of rot in production and what fixed it.

## What we don't want

- Marketing for your compression product.
- Vague claims with no source or numbers.
- Hot takes without a receipt attached.

## Format for a receipt

```
### Title of the work
- **Authors:** ...
- **Venue / date:** ...
- **Link:** ...
- **TL;DR:** 2–3 sentences. Lead with the key figure.
```

## Tone

Confident, sourced, honest. We pick a fight with an *idea* (compression-as-cramming),
never with people. Steelman before you strike.
